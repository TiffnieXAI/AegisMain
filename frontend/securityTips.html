Security Tips

Essential knowledge for staying safe in the blockchain ecosystem

Grandma Test

A.E.G.I.S. is designed to pass the "Grandma Test" - meaning our security warnings and explanations are clear enough that your grandmother could understand them. We believe security shouldn't require a computer science degree.

Remember: In Web3, there's no "undo button." Once a transaction is signed, it's permanent. That's why A.E.G.I.S. exists - to give you confidence and clarity before that final click.

Use Hardware Wallets

Use a hardware wallet for large balances and verify addresses on-device before signing.

Verify Contract Sources

Check verified contract code and audits before interacting with unknown contracts.

Double-Check Addresses

Always confirm recipient addresses are correct and consider using ENS names where available.

Beware Phishing Links

Never follow links from unknown messages; type trusted sites in the address bar directly.

Limit Token Approvals

Grant minimal approvals and revoke unused allowances regularly.

Keep Software Updated

Update your wallet and browser extensions to receive the latest security fixes.

Practice Caution with DApps

Test small amounts first and review transaction details before signing.

Common Web3 Threats

Phishing Attacks

Fake websites and contracts designed to steal your assets

Common Examples:

  • Fake wallet interfaces prompting for private keys
  • Impersonation of trusted services via email or social media
  • Malicious links leading to fake DApps

How to Protect:

  • Never enter private keys on unknown websites
  • Verify the authenticity of services before interacting
  • Avoid clicking suspicious links in emails or messages
